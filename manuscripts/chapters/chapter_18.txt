Chapter 18 - Picking Up the Spear 



“Change is on the horizon. Who are we to stand in its way?”









Her vision snapped back. The Prime was on the floor, tied up. Godric stood beside her with an embedded white Vitricite crystal spear pointed toward The Prime.

“Stone Face?” Praew called out.

“Good,” Godric said. “It worked.”

The Prime’s voice was raw and furious. “Godric, don’t tell me you truly believe that beast is still somehow human?”

Godric’s expression didn’t change. His grip didn’t waver. He simply sighed. “I don’t know about a beast. I just see how you’ve been treating my students. A crime punishable by death.”

The Prime choked in surprise. His eyes widened, his voice shaking. “You! You wouldn’t dare!”

Godric stabbed the spear blade down into the stone. While it stood upright, he released restraints and retrieved an EchoStone from behind her. “Come on,” he said. “We can’t stay here.”

Praew looked back at the stone slab. What happened to the BoltStone?

Her limbs felt weak, but she pushed herself up, forcing her body to move. They made their way out of the chamber, away from the awful stone slab.

Behind them, The Prime shouted, “Godric Rivers! You bring her back right now, or I will hunt you to the ends of the world!”

Godric didn’t stop. He exhaled. “Goodbye, brother.”

He pulled his Hunter’s cloak from his shoulders and draped it over Praew. “Wear this in case we get separated.”

She clutched the fabric, heart racing. He was taking her to the Second Floor? Toward the Gate to the surface?

Her mind reeled.

Godric’s voice snapped like a whip. “We need to leave the city, Praew. I know you’re worried about your parents, but—”

She gave an exhausted shrug. “There’s no reason to worry about them. They will live. If he kills them, I have no reason to return, right?”

Godric frowned, then nodded.

“We will get your parents out later,” he said. “If we can.”

Her breath was ragged. Don’t Hunters avoid traveling at night? No. Don’t think about that now. I must leave the city for now. And besides, I have Godric with me. I can finally go on that adventure I always dreamed of. I can handle this.

“You’ll be fine for now,” Godric said as they climbed toward the surface tunnels. “You’ll find it harder to breathe as you acclimate, but never stop running. You understand, girl?”

Praew swallowed hard and nodded.

Gate after gate, they climbed the slanted path. No guard dared question Silver Wing Godric. Each barrier opened the moment Godric approached.

Near the surface, something shifted. The air changed. She couldn’t tell if it was the BoltStone in her chest or the surface itself, but electricity surged. Her hair stood on end. A crackle hummed through the air.

Only one gate remained. A barred gate that teased Praew with a peek at her first sunrise. One more gate until freedom. A freedom that was now impossible.

Six Embedded warriors in identical masks stood between Praew and freedom. In front of them was Renwick with a smug grin on his face.

Praew’s heart sank.

It was over. At least I was able to breathe fresh air once. She tried to reassure herself, but—

Godric thrust his spear. The weapon launched toward the gate—then impact. A detonation of force. A surged ForceStone?

Praew barely had time to guess before the shockwave tore through the air. Renwick and the six Embedded warriors were scattered across the floor. And the gate? A human-sized hole gaped in its center.

Freedom.

Praew’s lungs gave out. She bent over, coughing. Couldn’t breathe.

Godric’s grip caught her arm, then flung her through the hole in the gate, up to the surface.

“Go!”

Praew staggered forward, then turned to look back at him.

She saw the back of her hero. The man she had idolized since childhood. Standing. Guarding the gate. Facing certain death, all to save her.

Something crushed her chest.

She had judged him so unfairly. Thought him emotionless. Though he showed the emotions of stone, he was truly a man who cared. He cared about his students, his city, and his people.

Why couldn’t you have been the Prime?

“Can’t you come with me?” she pleaded.

Godric didn’t turn. Didn’t hesitate. His voice was firm.

“Do you think you’re the only victim?”

The six Embedded were already rising to their feet.

Praew froze. She knew that quote. And finally, she understood.

Godric knew he might die here. He could probably escape if he left now.

But he wouldn’t. He couldn’t leave the city defenseless against The Prime’s cruelty.

He couldn’t live knowing he didn’t try.

Her vision blurred. Her feet moved. She ran.

But she turned back one last time. She had one thing left to say.

She cupped her hands and yelled, “Thank you for picking up your spear to face the storm!”

The words rang out over the battlefield. A quote from his book — Crescent Moon Srevlis. 

Godric’s stance didn’t falter. His grip on the spear tightened.

Then—

He raised it.

A silent goodbye.

Explosions erupted.

They followed her. No matter how far she ran.

Every step was a risk. Every breath a gamble.

Every sound, agony. Electricity crackled from the BoltStone in her chest, dancing across her skin, biting into her nerves. The ground was alive. The very act of running was testing fate.

She was triggering stones everywhere.

Her foot landed too hard on a ClamorStone, and thunder screamed back at her. A shockwave of sound exploded, ringing through her skull, reverberating in her bones. She gritted her teeth and kept moving.

A ZephyrStone howled as a gust slammed into her side. She skidded, nearly losing her footing, barely staying upright.

Her body felt stronger. She should’ve collapsed. Should’ve broken something.

She didn’t.

Her stamina was impossible. Her durability, inhuman. Did the BoltStone change me somehow?

Her electrical discharge grazed a ForceStone nearby. A pulse of pure force slammed into her ribs, launching her into the air. She tumbled, the impact knocking the breath from her lungs.

She landed gasping, limbs trembling, but nothing was broken.

A massive SolarStone filled the horizon. It radiated a pure, vengeful light, intensifying by the second. The glare pierced her vision, white-hot and searing. She squinted, but it was no use. She couldn’t see the stones anymore. Couldn’t see where she was running.

Splash.

An AquaStone. It shot a gush of water upward, catching her foot and launching her through the air. Limbs flailing, she crashed down hard onto a stone she had only heard about. A rush of gentle electricity stunned her in place, pinning her to the muddy ground. Her skin burned, her body screamed.

The ground split open as a TerraStone rumbled beneath her. A sinkhole attempted to swallow her, but she rolled out of the way. Rising to her feet, she continued to run.

Her lungs were failing. She was getting weaker.

Her limbs locked. Praew nearly collapsed.

She forced herself forward.

But the sun kept rising.

And with it, the world grew more violent.

The heat became unbearable. The glare blinding. She couldn’t see anything. She was stumbling blind, triggering stones she couldn’t even identify.

A ClamorStone shrieked. A BlastStone detonated. A ForceStone slammed her sideways.

Then — her BoltStone.

Zephyrs take me!

Electricity surged through her. Uncontrolled. Lightning arced from her skin. The pain paralyzed her. She couldn’t move.

She had become a walking storm.

The sun kept climbing, triggering more stones. More lightning. More agony.

She was going to die. Just like Junya and Wanni.

Here. After everything she’d gone through.

She had made it so far — and still, she was going to die.

At least I die free.

Praew closed her eyes, accepting her fate with a smile.

Back at his Grayparents’ home, Haldric knew this was coming. The explosions from the surface had made it clear: someone had made it to the surface. He just hoped it was Praew and Godric. Were they still alive? He didn’t have time to worry. The knock came moments later.

“Special Forces! Open up!”

Even expecting it, his heart raced. He didn’t resist. There was no point. He doubted The Prime would send two ordinary enforcers. Haldric guessed they were Embedded, their stones well-hidden, or acclimated at the very least. If he ran, he wouldn’t make it three steps.

As they led him through the corridors, his eyes flicked toward their necks. Burn scars. Similar to the documents in The Prime’s office. The recorrection program. Just how many people does The Prime control? Can I free them somehow?

 Haldric knew he was only trying to keep his mind busy to avoid drowning in the guilt for letting those violent prisoners free. That poor woman. This poor city.

After the familiar trek, Haldric and his escorts neared the Administration building.

He should’ve been afraid. But he wasn’t. He already knew how this would go. Haldric would deny everything. The Prime would see through it, of course, but he’d forgive him anyway. Just long enough to justify his own despicable actions. That bolting man.

Haldric clenched his fists as they reached The Prime’s office. The doors swung open.

The Prime Archon stood inside, hands clasped behind his back, not even looking at him yet. 	Haldric stepped forward, already forming his excuse.

“I—”

CRACK.

He lurched. The backhanded blow struck before he even processed it. He hit the floor, pain exploding through his cheek as the cold stone scraped against his side. Too fast.

A boot slammed into his ribs.

“You idiot!”

Another kick.

“Boy!”

Another.

“You doomed us all!”

Haldric gasped, curling in on himself as another vicious strike knocked the wind from his lungs. His ears rang, his body screamed, but the worst part wasn’t the pain—it was the tone in The Prime’s voice. Not just rage. Not just betrayal.

Pure disappointment.

Another kick, harder this time.

“Why?” Kick. “Must you?” Kick. “Ruin everything?”

Haldric pressed his hands to the floor, trying to rise. The Prime’s shadow loomed over him, breath rough, shoulders shaking. Then he stilled.

“Fool! You terrorized the whole city, releasing every criminal in our jail!?” His voice trembled with barely restrained fury. “Now I have to imprison my own brother! Why did you have to involve him!?”

Haldric barely had time to react before the next words struck harder than any blow.

“Bresdin, you even released Bresdin.”

Silence. The temperature in the room dropped.

The Prime wasn’t just furious anymore. He was afraid. For the first time since Haldric had met him, Arthur Rivers looked shaken. He stepped forward again, raising his foot for another blow.

Haldric tensed, bracing—

“Father, please!”

The words spilled out before he could stop them. 

He wasn’t even my father. Why did I call this monster that?

The Prime froze. The kick never came.

A long silence followed. Then he sighed, dragging a hand down his face. “I… lost my temper.” His voice was calm now, almost tired. Then he crouched down, voice soft.

“Son, are you alright?”

***



Godric’s breath burned, each pull of air sharp and shallow, ribs screaming with every movement. Blood ran warm beneath his armor, soaking into fabric and leather alike.

 He forced his arm to move and drove the butt of his spear into the stone to keep himself upright. The impact rang through his arms, draining what little strength remained. His knee struck the ground a heartbeat later. Scaled leather scraped against the wet stone as he knelt.

This was reckless. When did I pick up habits from those bolting kids?

Godric couldn’t move.

His body no longer listened to him.

How long had it been since he felt fear like this?

Do you think you’re the only victim?

The line echoed in his mind.

How would that bolting girl misinterpret my words this time?

Godric’s shoulder spasmed. His fingers twitched against the spearshaft before going still.

Perhaps he shouldn’t be so hard on her. He’d likely done the same thing, misunderstanding when Srevlis asked him the same question.

The Terra and Zephyr users slowly closed in, footsteps light, as deadly as they were dead inside.

Lurking behind them, the Echo user laughed.

“I’d ask if you had any final words,” he said, voice light with amusement, “but I won’t bother remembering them.”

Godric’s grip loosened around the spear. Just a little.

“How poetic,” the Echo brat continued. “I can’t think of a better way to finish you than letting these two do it.”

He gestured lazily toward the warriors ahead of him.

Godric’s head dipped forward. Stone filled his vision before his eyes finally closed.

Hopefully Praew didn’t make me out to be some bolting hero.

A cough tore out of him.

No, she’ll hate me instead.

That was better. It almost made him smile.



*** 



Half a flash ago.





Godric hurled Praew toward the opening. She cleared the breach and struck ground hard on the other side.

 He sprinted to the opening, then turned his back on it to face the warriors again.

Because you never took your eyes off an opponent.

Their leader wasn’t moving.

Good, stay down.

 The six masked warriors were slowly rising, moving in perfect unison, completely unaffected by the earlier blast. Well… not quite. The ceramic masks that had held through the impact, begun to fail under motion.

Cracks spread as they moved, thin fractures crawling across ceramic. Revealing only pieces of their face.

There was something about that one’s mouth and cheekbone.

Why did that look like—

And that other one’s eye and nose felt familiar as well.

His mind began to count.

Junya, on the floor of the recorrection chamber, deceased.

Praew, behind him, through the gate.

Two still missing.

He stayed facing them, adjusted his footing, keeping all six in view. 

As if to confirm his fear, one mask crumbled further, as its wearer straightened. Most of it ripping free until only a jagged ring clung around his eye.

The other shattered outright, fragments scattering across the stone.

Jorpen and Wanni.

All of the missing students accounted for.

“Can’t you come with me?” Praew pleaded from behind him.

“Do you think you’re the only victim?”

Godric hesitated, the words already regretted.

If she saw her classmates, she would never leave.

“Thank you!” Praew called out. She said more, but the words blurred together, lost to distance and ringing air.

He lifted his spear as a final signal. “Get moving.”



***



Six Embedded warriors began to flank their prey.

Godric held his ground, eyes locked on as many as possible. Though never letting their movement slip from the edge of his vision, he couldn’t take his eyes off his greatest shame, a reminder that made the back of his own neck throb.

His students were standing here because of it.

Wanni and Jorpen stared at him with blank eyes.

Godric’s grip tightened, knuckles whitening against the vitracite spear.

They would track down Praew using her compassion against her. Even if she escaped, it would change her. She would view her compassion as weakness. 

The greatest weakness my brother and I share is that we made the same mistake.

While growing more calluses, we grew more callous.

Bolting girl. For the sake of this world, You can not be allowed to forsake your greatest strength. 

I will do whatever it takes to prevent it. 

And for that, Wanni and Jorpen couldn’t be left alive.

A soft voice cut through the silence.

“Why didn’t you save us, professor?” Wanni asked. 

Godric didn’t falter.

The sound seemed to come from her but there was no movement from her mouth.

Their leader sat up from where he lay and grinned.

Godric exhaled through his nose. “Embedded with an EchoStone, eh?” His voice stayed flat. “Those bolting tricks won’t work on me, kid.”

Their leader tilted his head. “Oh? Don’t be so sure about that.” He chuckled, voice lowering mid-laugh.

“Now,” he said in The Prime’s insufferable voice, “kill him.”

Something cold settled in Godric’s chest as the warriors slowly closed in.

Renwick lingered behind them, watching, smiling, content to let his slaves do the dirty work.

That echo brat must be dealt with as well.

Godric took a long deep breath, his ritual before any battle.

He slid on his embedded gloves. A NullStone embedded into the palm of his left, A SurgeStone set into the back of his right. His fingers curled into a fist. The stones pulsed—one ready to silence, the other ready to amplify.

One warrior’s veins blackened beneath his skin, crawling up his forearms as power flooded his body. 

SurgeStone

Godric  rolled his shoulders, shifting his spear into position.

“Come on, then.”

The attack came fast. The Surge user lunged in close, fists driving forward with crushing blows. Each strike heavy enough to shatter stone. Godric met him head-on, spear braced, absorbing the strikes, letting the impact rattle through his arms without yielding ground. Though each blow was heavy, the surge user’s movements were too predictable.

Jorpen tilted his head back.

Spitting jagged shards of stone into the sky.

TerraStone

 Wanni moved in the same breath.

She jumped ridiculously high.

ZephyrStone

Wanni swung her arms towards the floating stone shards. Wind howled as the ZephyrStone answered, a violent downward blast slamming into the rising shrapnel. The sharp stones reversed direction in an instant, whipped downward toward Godric in a lethal cloud.

Godric surged the ForceStone embedded in his spear and drove the weapon forward. Force detonated outward in a focused blast, hurling the surging warrior backward across the stone floor. The body skidded, slammed the city wall, and vanished from his immediate concern.

Godric spun.

He lifted the spear overhead and rotated it faster and faster. The spear became a white blur above his head, vitracite singing as stone shattered against it bouncing off the whistling shaft. 

The wind dulled as the last fragments bounced and skidded across the ground. 

A compressed blast of surged force launched toward him from the side. Godric twisted and snapped his left hand out to catch it, crushing the blast under his grip with the aid of the NullStone. The blast vanished in his palm with a deafening crack. All of Stormhaven shuddered beneath his feet.

ForceStone

Pain flared through Godric’s foot, the shockwave tearing open the wound from the day before. His leg wavered. He faltered.

An electrically charged palm slammed into Godric’s back.

BoltStone

The current froze him where he stood.

A poorly aimed volley of surged force just missed Godric’s head, slamming into the vitricite spearhead and detonating on impact.

Sound vanished. Then it came back screaming, a high, relentless ring that drowned his thoughts. His vision swam, the Force user ahead of him doubling, tripling, approaching.

The BoltStone user was caught in the blast. Their hand broke contact.

Godric surged himself.

The current crashed back into him as the Bolt user made contact again.

Too late.

The Force user sprang forward, arm outstretched, aiming a direct contact blast at his face.

Black veins crept up Godric’s arms.

Clarity followed.

The Embedded barely had time to register what happened before Godric snapped his left hand out, catching the warrior’s arm and redirecting the readied burst of compressed force to the right side of the battlefield. 

The blast clipped the Surge user’s shoulder and sent him crashing down. Godric jerked his spear backward at an angle, driving the butt into the Bolt user’s gut. The man stumbled backward with a gasp, breaking the electrical current.

Without pausing, Godric loosened his grip just enough to slide his hand toward the spear’s head, shortening its reach. He rammed the angled blade through the Force user’s upper abdomen on the right side. Stepping back, he seized the shaft with both hands and shifted his weight. He pushed down with his left side, pulled with his right, and twisted through his hips. The spear sheared through the spine and burst free just above the corpse’s left hip.

The body split apart. The stench of blood and bowels flooded the air as it collapsed.

That’s one

A sharp pain flared along the back of Godric’s right leg. It buckled under the fresh gash.

He spun just in time to see a bare-footed, dual-blade wielder slide past him, leaving a streak of wet stone in her wake.

She flicked his blood from the blades, settled into an aggressive stance, then advanced. The first strike glanced off his spear. The second forced him back a step.

A side kick followed.

It didn’t reach him.

Godric didn’t notice the embedded Stone in her heel until it was too late.

A jet of water detonated forward, striking his center mass and hurling him across the stone. 

AquaStone

He skidded hard, boots screaming, and came to a stop in the center of the battlefield—exposed.

The others began to circle.

Learning.

Watching.

The Surge user advanced. Godric saw it coming, but there was no dodging this one. The blow landed clean. A fist infused with surged energy struck his side.

CRACK.

Godric’s ribs shattered.

The world tilted, but he caught himself. 

He pulled an EchoStone from his pouch, gripping it in his left palm.

His body screamed for him to drop, to kneel, to collapse into the dirt.

His breath burned, each pull of air sharp and shallow, ribs screaming with every movement. Blood ran warm beneath his armor, soaking into fabric and leather alike.

 He forced his arm to move and drove the butt of his spear into the stone to keep himself upright. The impact rang through his arms, draining what little strength remained. His knee struck the ground a heartbeat later. Scaled leather scraped against the wet stone as he knelt.

This was reckless. When did I pick up habits from those bolting kids?

Godric couldn’t move.

His body no longer listened to him.

A surged BoltStone, further amplified by the wet stone beneath him, locked Godric in place.

How long had it been since he felt fear like this?

He drifted in his thoughts as Wanni and—no, the Terra and Zephyr users—closed in.

Godric’s head dipped forward. Stone filled his vision before his eyes finally closed.

They stopped directly in front of him. Moments from ending his life.

“May they find comfort in death,” Godric murmured. “I pray you grant them more mercy in their next life. Amen.”

He triggered the EchStone in his hand.

“Stop, at once!” The Prime’s commanding voice rang out.

The BoltStone ceased its surge. Every warrior froze.

In one fluid motion, Godric rose and swept his spear in a wide horizontal arc. Two heads struck the stone floor with a wet slap.

Jorpen and Wanni.

I’m sorry.

As their bodies began to fall, something sharp slid across Godric’s neck. Blood followed. Too much blood.

He surged himself.

Turning, Godric found the EchoStone user directly behind him, blade still raised, grinning. His vision blurred as breath failed him. The world felt submerged.

How was the boy unaffected?

 Godric staggered forward and seized the EchoStone user by the back of the neck, bracing himself against the boy’s shoulder to remain standing.

“How?” The Echo user’s eyes widened as his grin vanished. “Why?” He stammered. “Why do you not fall?”

Godric jerked his spear back, gripping the very tip of the shaft. He shoved it forward blindly. The spearhead struck teeth before finding the boy’s mouth.

Godric triggered a surged blast of force.

The EchoStone user collapsed.

Four down.

The remaining three Embedded Warriors stood frozen, held in place by The Prime’s own command. They couldn’t move.

It would have to be enough.

Godric turned toward the breach in the gate.

Each step dragged. His legs barely listened anymore, muscles trembling under the strain of surging long past his limits. Pain began to dull, slipping away with every movement.

He reached the opening.

Cold air spilled through the shattered gate, carrying the scent of mossy dew. 

So bolting cold.

Godric stepped through.

The world opened before him.

Wind brushed through his hair. He lifted his head, eyes tracing the open sky above Ravour.

No storm this morning. How peaceful.

For a moment, he stood there.

Then, with what little strength remained, Godric drove his spear into the stone with a grinding crunch.

The spear shuddered as the shaft bit deep into the ground. He leaned into it, shoulders sagging, weight settling.

His head bowed.

The world dimmed.
